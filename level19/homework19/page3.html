Hanging Gardens of Babylon

The Hanging Gardens of Babylon is the only one of the Seven Wonders of the World whose exact location is unclear.

There are no written sources, so there are three versions about their existence:

first:
the first claims that it is a mythical object described only in Greek and Roman source

second:
the second indicates that the gardens really existed and were most likely completely destroyed by an earthquake in the 1st century.

third: A third theory says that the legend confused the locations and actually referred to the well-documented gardens of the Assyrian king Sennacherib (745-681 BC) in Nineveh, where the king laid out gardens for his young wife, a princess who, in the dusty and arid land,longed for her homeland of green hills and forests.
